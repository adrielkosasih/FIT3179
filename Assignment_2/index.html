Life Expectancy Across the World

2000-2015

The number of years a person may expect to live is referred to as "life expectancy." By definition, life expectancy is based on a prediction of the average age at which members of a demographic group would die. The difference between cohort and period life expectancy is an essential distinction and clarification. Life expectancy in a given year may be thought of as the average age at which a person born that year would expect to die if the average age of death did not vary during their lifetime. An alternate method involves determining the average duration of life for a hypothetical cohort that was exposed to the mortality rates recorded at one point in time from birth to death.

Overall Life Expectancy Across the World

To begin, we will look at the global trend in life expectancy. The darker region surrounding Japan in the year 2000 shows the country's high life expectancy - an average of 81.1 years old.

Throughout the year, we can observe that the majority of nations' life expectancies are rising. Japan is no longer the country with the greatest life expectancy in 2007. With a life expectancy of 89, France, Spain, and Sweden have become the countries with the greatest life expectancy.

When we change the year to 2015, we can see that the three countries mentioned earlier are no longer among the top five countries. Slovenia is currently the number one country, with an average life expectancy of 88 years, followed by Denmark with an average life expectancy of 86 years.

Average Life Expectancy Per Region

We can see that Europe has consistently had the highest life expectancy throughout the year. and, in second place, Americas.

Most regions' life expectancy continues to rise throughout the year. With the exception of Oceania. In the Oceania region, life expectancy is increasing year by year till 2009. Oceania's life expectancy is lower in 2010 than it was in 2009. As a result, from 2010 to 2015, Oceania has been ranked lower than Asia.

In comparison to the other four regions, Africa has a relatively low life expectancy. Between 2000 and 2015, the overall average was 58.86 years. The average life expectancy in four other regions exceeds 70 years.

Average Life Expectancy per Country Type (Developed or Developing)

Year after year, both Country types exhibit an upward trend.

Despite the fact that life expectancy in developing countries is growing every year. Their best life expectancy still falls short of that of developed nations in the year 2000, the first year the data was collected. The average life expectancy of developed countries was 76.8 years in 2000, whereas the average life expectancy of developing countries was 69.69 years in 2015, the most recent year for which data was available.

After 15 years, developing nations' average life expectancy has grown by approximately 5%, whereas developing countries' has increased by around 8%. In comparison to developed countries, developing countries have a greater rate of growth.
